# Climate Change: Causes, Effects, and Solutions

## Overview

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in global temperatures and weather patterns. While some climate change is natural, since the mid-20th century human activities have been the primary driver of climate change.

## Primary Causes

### Greenhouse Gas Emissions
- **Carbon Dioxide (CO2)**: Released by burning fossil fuels (coal, oil, natural gas) and deforestation
- **Methane (CH4)**: Produced by livestock, rice paddies, and landfills
- **Nitrous Oxide (N2O)**: Emitted from agricultural soils and fertilizers

### Deforestation
Forests absorb CO2. When they are cut down, not only is this carbon sink lost, but the stored carbon is released back into the atmosphere.

## Key Effects

### Rising Temperatures
The global average temperature has risen by approximately 1.1°C since the pre-industrial period. Without action, it could rise by 3–4°C by 2100.

### Sea Level Rise
Melting polar ice and thermal expansion of oceans are causing sea levels to rise. Low-lying areas and island nations face existential threats.

### Extreme Weather Events
Increased frequency and intensity of:
- Hurricanes and typhoons
- Droughts and heatwaves
- Flooding and storms

### Biodiversity Loss
Many species cannot adapt quickly enough to changing conditions, leading to extinction and ecosystem collapse.

## Solutions

### Renewable Energy
Transitioning from fossil fuels to solar, wind, and hydroelectric power is the single most impactful solution.

### Energy Efficiency
Improving insulation, electric vehicles, and efficient appliances can significantly reduce emissions.

### Carbon Capture
Technologies that remove CO2 directly from the atmosphere are being developed and deployed.

### Policy Measures
- Carbon pricing and taxes
- International agreements (e.g., Paris Agreement)
- Renewable energy subsidies

## The Paris Agreement

Adopted in 2015, the Paris Agreement commits nations to limit global warming to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels, with efforts to limit it to 1.5°C.
